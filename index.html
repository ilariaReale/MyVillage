The Problem

Modern life broke the village.

Three separate crises — unaffordable childcare, senior isolation, and fragmented neighborhoods — are actually one problem: we lost the village.

Childcare is unaffordable

$30–$50/hr for a babysitter. Over $3,500/month for two kids in daycare. Families are stretched to the breaking point.

Seniors are isolated

Friends pass away. Children move cities. Seniors have time, wisdom, and love to give — but no infrastructure to share it.

Immigrants raise kids alone

Grandparents are on another continent. No aunts, no cousins, no family safety net. The village that should form naturally — doesn't.

Neighbors are strangers

We live next door for years without knowing each other's names. Real community doesn't happen by accident anymore.

Our Vision

More than an app. A new kind of community infrastructure.

Think of it as the digital version of how communities used to work — word of mouth, trust, reciprocity. Where you could knock on a neighbor's door and ask for help. Where an elder was a resource, not a burden.

We're building the platform that makes that possible again — at scale, across cities, for everyone who's ever felt that raising kids or growing old shouldn't be this lonely.

The Solution

Built on who you are, not what you do.

Your profile reflects your passions, origins, availability, and what you can offer. The app finds genuine matches nearby — not transactions, but real relationships.

Discover Neighbors

Browse nearby families, seniors, and individuals. Get a "neighbor who'd love to meet you" suggestion every day.

Village Events

Create and join local events — Swaps, Strolls, Playdates, Meals, Garden days. Meet in real life, not just online.

Direct Messaging

Chat directly with your neighbors. Coordinate a cooking session, a pickup, a shared afternoon — naturally.

Village Score

Track your connections and offers made. A reputation system built on generosity, not followers.

Who It's For

Four people. One village.

My Village brings together people who have complementary needs and turns them into a real community.

Families

Parents who need childcare support, playdates, and community — without paying a fortune for strangers.

Seniors

Retirees with time, warmth, and skills to share — looking for purpose and genuine human connection.

Individuals

People new to a city, navigating a life transition, or simply wanting to put down roots in their neighborhood.

Caregivers

People who offer help — cooking, language exchange, babysitting, errands — and want to be part of a trusted network.
