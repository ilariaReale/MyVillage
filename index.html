The Problem

Modern life broke the village.
We want to rebuild it.

Families pay a fortune for childcare from strangers. Seniors lose their friends and sit alone. Immigrants raise children thousands of miles from their own parents. And neighbors don't know each other's names. These aren't separate problems — they're one.

💸

Childcare is unaffordable

$30–$50/hr for a babysitter. Over $4,000/month for daycare. Families are stretched to the limit.

🧓

Seniors are isolated

Friends pass away. Children move. Seniors have time, wisdom, and love to give — but no way to connect.

🌍

Families are far apart

Grandparents are on another continent. The bonds that used to hold communities together are missing.

🏘️

Neighborhoods feel empty

We live next door to each other but don't know each other. Real community doesn't happen by accident anymore.

How It Works

A LinkedIn for community — built on who you are, not what you do.

Instead of a work resume, your profile highlights your passions, cultural background, availability, and what you need. The app finds genuine matches in your neighborhood — and helps you build real relationships over time.

1

Create your profile

Share who you are — your origins, languages, passions, what you can offer, and what you're looking for.

2

Get matched locally

We connect you with compatible people nearby — a senior who loves kids, a neighbor who speaks your language, a family that needs what you offer.

3

Start small, grow real

A first meeting, a shared meal, an afternoon with the kids. Relationships that are genuine — not transactional.

4

Build your village

Over time, you gain a network of trusted neighbors — reducing costs, loneliness, and the feeling of doing it all alone.

Our Vision

More than childcare. A new kind of community infrastructure.

Think of it as the digital version of how communities used to work — word of mouth, trust, reciprocity. Where you could knock on a neighbor's door and ask for help. Where an elder was a resource, not a burden.

We're building a platform that scales that feeling. Whether it's a "grandparent" who watches the kids on weekends, a neighbor who cooks traditional food for an elderly man who misses home, or a teenager who helps a senior use a smartphone — every connection matters.
